# KYB Aggregator Research — Startup-Friendly Coverage for Strale

**Research date:** 2026-04-21
**Scope:** Commercial KYB aggregators for EU (18 long-tail jurisdictions) + UK + Norway + US, evaluated against a pre-revenue Swedish AB (Moonlighter AB) with public "direct data connections, no scraping" positioning.
**Method:** three parallel researchers (US-forward/agentic, European/global incumbents, compliance/specialty/regional), output merged here with coverage + ranking tables built centrally.
**Status of claims:** pricing is tagged inline as `[published]`, `[practitioner estimate]`, or `[contact sales required]`. Every contract-level figure needs live verification in commercial outreach — see §S.3.

## Cobalt Intelligence

### Provider identity
- **HQ:** Scottsdale, Arizona, USA
- **Founded:** 2020 (founder: Jordan Hansen)
- **Public customers:** Not prominently published; smaller startup-stage roster (lenders, AP automation vendors).
- **Business model:** Per-call API for US Secretary of State scraping and lien/UCC lookups. Self-serve with published pricing.

### Country coverage for Strale's target list
Explicitly US-only per marketing (https://www.cobaltintelligence.com/secretary-of-state-api). Blog/changelog through April 2026 shows no non-US launch. Late-2023 post mentioned Canada provinces as roadmap; no announcement has followed.

- **US:** direct (scraping of state SoS portals)
- 18 EU countries: not covered

### Pricing — exhaustive
- **Entry tier:** Published self-serve: $0.50–$1.00 per Secretary of State search at low volume [published] (cobaltintelligence.com/pricing).
- **500/mo:** ~$250/mo [published].
- **5k/mo:** ~$0.25–0.40 [practitioner estimate].
- **50k/mo:** likely $0.10–0.20 [practitioner estimate].
- **Upfront fees:** none advertised.
- **Min commit:** pay-as-you-go available — **strong startup fit** [published].
- **Free tier:** 10 free searches on signup [published].
- **Volume discounts:** tiered automatically.

**Caveat:** Cobalt scrapes Secretary of State portals. Strale's "direct data connections, no scraping" public claim creates a positioning conflict — either reconcile (SoS portals are the authoritative government source; Cobalt mechanizes access) or disclose aggregator use.

### Redistribution rights
Cobalt TOS permits embedding results in customer-facing products. Attribution not required. Wholesale dumps prohibited. Favorable for Strale's model.

### Onboarding for a Swedish AB
Self-serve via Stripe. No entity-residency restrictions. Immediate.

### Data fields returned
Legal name ✓, registry ID (state file) ✓, legal form ✓, address ✓, status ✓, formation date ✓, VAT ✗ (US has no VAT), directors/officers ✓ (varies by state), UBO ✗ (FinCEN BOI separate), NAICS ✗.

### API shape
REST, API key, webhooks available, rate limits ~10 req/sec at standard tier, no formal SLA published.

### Startup-friendliness rating: 🟢
Published pricing, month-to-month, free trial, self-serve, no entity restrictions.

### Recommendation
Viable for US-only. Shortlist for Strale's US-50 need. Pre-commit: (1) confirm redistribution language in writing; (2) confirm coverage depth for officers/agents per state (some states redact); (3) reconcile scraping method with Strale's "no scraping" positioning. Outreach Qs: latency p95 per state, handling of state portal outages, bulk batch endpoints.

## govfiles.dev

### Provider identity
- **HQ:** not publicly disclosed; developer-tools brand
- **Founded:** 2023 (inferred from domain reg + HN launch posts)
- **Public customers:** not listed; small customer base typical of 2023–24 startup

- **500/mo:** ~$125–250 [published].

- **50k/mo:** contact sales [contact sales required].
- **Upfront fees:** none.

- **Free tier:** 100 free calls on signup [published].

### Redistribution rights — ODbL DEEP DIVE
OpenCorporates data is **Open Database License (ODbL) 1.0** for free tier (https://opencorporates.com/info/our-data/). Commercial contracts override ODbL with negotiated terms. ODbL analysis matters because "open data ≠ free to embed."

**Three ODbL clauses affecting Strale:**

1. **Attribution (§4.2):** Public use must attribute OC with visible URL. For Payee Assurance reports, every report with OC data needs visible attribution — acceptable but must be designed in.

2. **Share-Alike (§4.4):** Any "Derivative Database" must be ODbL-licensed. Thorny clause. If Strale produces a downstream "Produced Work" (PDF report, JSON response) *from* OC data, the Produced Work does NOT need to be ODbL. However, if Strale *stores* OC data, *enriches* it, and re-serves it *via API as a database*, that enhanced database arguably triggers Share-Alike.

3. **Produced Work clause (§4.3):** Explicit exemption — "Produced Works" (reports, analyses, visualizations) are not themselves ODbL-subject. Strale's customer-facing KYB report likely qualifies as a Produced Work, NOT a derivative database.

**Practical verdict for Strale:** Using the free tier in production is defensible **only if**:
- Strale treats OC data as ephemeral "Produced Works" (individual KYB reports), not persistently storing/re-serving as queryable database
- Attribution visibly displayed on every customer-facing output
- Strale does not resell OC data in bulk or as a queryable API

Given Strale's agent-to-agent model, where the consuming agent may cache and re-query, the line between "Produced Work" and "Derivative Database" becomes blurry. **Commercial licence strongly recommended** to avoid ambiguity. Refs: ODbL full text https://opendatacommons.org/licenses/odbl/1-0/ + OC FAQ https://opencorporates.com/info/our-data/.

The public "no scraping, direct data" claim Strale makes is **also a concern**: OpenCorporates obtains most of its data by scraping registries. Strale relabeling OC-derived data as "direct" would be misleading.

# Synthesis and recommendation

## S.1 — Startup-friendly shortlist

Top 3 aggregators ranked by startup-friendliness across pricing, onboarding, redistribution, and coverage:

1. **Kyckr** — broadest direct EU-registry coverage (all 18 target countries) with explicit redistribution rights for regulated KYB/CDD, no minimum commitment, self-service signup. Pay-per-document pass-through is expensive per-hit for DE/IT/ES (€10–30) but honest and predictable — Strale absorbs the same cost a direct registry contract would charge. Single biggest risk: post-acquisition entity may have tightened terms since 2023 — verify current self-service access exists.

2. **Openapi.it** — near-perfect architectural fit (Italian S.p.A., prepaid credits, no commitment, explicit KYB/KYC permitted use, transparent per-call pricing) and direct coverage for Italy via InfoCamere licensing. EBR gateway extends to other EU with per-country pricing. For Italian coverage specifically, this is likely the single best vendor for Strale. Outside Italy, depth varies; verify per-country.

3. **North Data** — self-service, €50/mo entry, month-to-month, clear redistribution, and the most usable German corporate data aggregator below enterprise tier. Covers DE + AT (and CH, which Strale already has). Pair with Kyckr or Openapi.it for wider EU. Better signal than Handelsregister.ai with clearer legal footing and redistribution rights.

**Honorable mention:** **Informa D&B** for ES + PT specifically via eInforma PAYG — startup-friendlier than parent D&B for Iberia with negotiable carve-out redistribution.

## S.2 — Coverage strategy

**Recommended pattern: C — Long-tail hybrid.** Direct government APIs where Strale already has them (10 countries today, plus GR + HR in progress) + targeted aggregator mix for everything else.

Rationale:
- Matches Strale's public "direct data connections, no scraping" positioning more honestly than Patterns A or B — every additional direct government integration preserves that brand claim.
- Lowest per-call cost at low volume: direct APIs are usually either free (Companies House UK, Brreg NO, Bolagsverket SE, Zefix CH) or low-cost (KvK NL, Firmenbuch AT direct); aggregator fees only apply to the long tail.
- Scales with Strale's actual product: the first 500 KYB calls/mo are probably concentrated in Nordic + top-EU jurisdictions Strale already has; aggregator exposure stays small.
- Vendor-dependency risk distributed across 3–4 small aggregators rather than one large one. If Kyckr tightens terms, Openapi.it covers IT; if Openapi.it raises EBR price, Kyckr covers IT directly.

Effort estimate: 2–4 weeks to wire in Kyckr (pay-per-document, one REST integration, then document extraction pipeline) + 1–2 weeks Openapi.it + 1 week North Data + 1 week Informa D&B (Iberia) = **~6–8 weeks of engineering** for full 18-EU coverage via aggregators. US adds another 1–2 weeks to wire Cobalt and/or govfiles.dev.

Cost at 500/mo volume (mixed country profile, 60% Nordic/UK/FR which are direct, 40% aggregator-intermediated):
- Direct APIs: €0 most; Companies House UK, CZ ARES, AU ABR free; KVK-equivalents €6–10/month + €0.02/call
- Kyckr for long tail (assume 80 calls/mo to DE/IT/ES mix @ €15 avg): ~€1,200/mo
- Openapi.it for IT-heavy traffic (assume 40 calls to IT @ €3): ~€120/mo
- North Data for DE (40 calls @ €1): ~€40/mo
- Informa D&B for ES/PT (40 calls @ €7): ~€280/mo
- **Total ~€1,600/mo at 500 calls/mo** (blended ~€3.20/call)

At 5k/mo, blended probably lands ~€1.20–1.80/call with volume discounts — competitive with any single aggregator.

Time to launch: 6–8 weeks for full coverage vs 3–4 weeks for a single aggregator (Pattern A). The extra 3–4 weeks buys lower lock-in, lower unit cost at scale, and brand consistency with the "direct data" claim.

**Pattern A (single aggregator)** — only viable vendor that could do all 18 EU + US in one contract is Trulioo, Moody's Orbis, or D&B — all 🔴 for Strale. Skip Pattern A.

**Pattern B (regional split)** — Creditsafe for EU + Cobalt for US. Works, but Creditsafe's 12-month commit + rider friction + no real sandbox at a minimum above €8k/yr are exactly the commercial barriers Strale was trying to avoid. Fall back to B only if C's multi-vendor integration complexity is a blocker.

## S.3 — Reality check on pricing assumptions

Two prior research passes on this topic (EU, US) made pricing claims that turned out partially wrong when tested against live registration (KVK's "free HVDS" was actually €6.40/mo + €0.02/call; Cobalt's "PAYG with no minimum" had shifted to monthly subscription). So the commercial-outreach team needs a clear view of which numbers in this research are vendor-published vs practitioner-estimated vs sales-gated.

**Verify in commercial outreach FIRST (sales-gated or estimate-based, high impact on decision):**

- **Kyckr per-document pricing for DE, IT, ES, NL, BE** — pricing page often login-gated; estimates based on pass-through from registries. If Kyckr adds a per-call markup above the registry's own fee, unit economics change significantly.
- **Creditsafe annual minimum for Swedish AB** — £6–12k is a practitioner estimate, Vendr data; Stockholm office may offer a lower "developer" tier that isn't advertised. Ask explicitly.
- **Kompany post-Moody's pricing** — the biggest uncertainty in this research. Pre-acquisition Kompany was PAYG-friendly; post-acquisition is reportedly $50k+ minimums but individual sales reps may still honor lower commits for non-US startups. Worth a 30-min call to test.
- **Persona KYB-only pricing** — their public pricing is KYC + KYB bundles. Ask for pure-KYB SKU and for the platform amendment cost for B2B2B.
- **Trulioo structured pilot** — explicitly ask if they'd do a structured 6-month pilot <€15k. Answer probably no, but worth confirming.
- **Cobalt PAYG still available** — given the prior-research drift from PAYG to monthly subscription, verify current TOS before integration planning.

**Verify in writing even though published (easy to get, important):**

- Redistribution language for Cobalt and govfiles.dev — their TOS is permissive but Strale's MCP/agent model is unusual enough to want explicit confirmation.
- OpenCorporates commercial tier — free tier is ODbL-ambiguous; confirm commercial licence is needed for Strale's model and confirm price.
- North Data MCP/agent redistribution — published terms cover customer-facing products but MCP is novel.
- Openapi.it agent-to-agent redistribution in writing.

**Numbers most likely reliable (vendor-published, stable):**

- Dilisense pricing (Starter €49/mo for 1k) — transparent, stable model.
- govfiles.dev published per-call bands.
- Cobalt's published per-call bands.
- North Data's published €50/mo entry tier.
- Openapi.it's published per-country prices.
- Handelsregister.ai's published per-call pricing (caveat: the *data legality* is the risk, not the price).

**Numbers least reliable (practitioner estimates from Vendr / forums, high-impact decisions):**

- All enterprise minimums for D&B, Experian, Equifax, Socure, Trulioo, LexisNexis, Sayari, Refinitiv, Moody's Orbis, Encompass, Neotas — estimates span wide ranges; actual minimums depend on negotiation leverage, region, and timing. Most are "north of €25k" which is enough to disqualify without exact figures. Don't prioritize verification.
- Bisnode / Enento API floors — less Vendr data available for Nordic-regional vendors.
- Kyckr actual list prices at current self-service tier (login-gated).
